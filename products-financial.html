GASB Compliance

OpenGov Financials provides complete support for all relevant Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) pronouncements, ensuring your financial reporting meets the highest standards of transparency and accountability.

GASB 54: Fund Balance Reporting

Required in All States

What it requires: State and local governments must classify fund balances into five categories: Nonspendable, Restricted, Committed, Assigned, and Unassigned.

üöÄ OpenGov Solution

Our system automatically tracks and classifies fund balances according to GASB 54 requirements. Generate compliant financial statements with one click, including detailed fund balance schedules and notes disclosures.

Key Features:

  • Automated fund balance classification
  • Real-time fund balance monitoring
  • GASB 54-compliant financial statements
  • Fund balance policy tracking
  • Detailed notes disclosures generation

GASB 34: Infrastructure Reporting

Full Support

What it requires: Governments must report infrastructure assets and depreciation, with options for modified approach based on condition assessment.

üöÄ OpenGov Solution

Integrated fixed asset management tracks infrastructure assets, calculates depreciation, and generates required disclosures. Supports both depreciation and modified approaches for infrastructure reporting.

Other GASB Standards Supported

Single Audit Support

For governments expending $750,000 or more in federal awards annually, OpenGov Financials provides comprehensive Single Audit support in accordance with 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance).

Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA)

Automated Generation

The SEFA is a critical component of the Single Audit, listing all federal awards expended during the fiscal year, organized by federal agency and program (CFDA number).

üöÄ OpenGov Solution

Automatically generate SEFA reports with proper formatting, including federal agency, CFDA numbers, program names, pass-through information, and expenditure amounts. Built-in validation ensures accuracy and compliance.

What We Track:

  • Federal grantor agency
  • CFDA (Catalog of Federal Domestic Assistance) numbers
  • Federal program names
  • Pass-through entity information
  • Pass-through identifying numbers
  • Federal expenditures by program
  • Major program determination
  • Type A/Type B program classification
